About me

Hi, I'm Sofia.

I'm a Chemistry student at Yale University (Class of 2029), concentrating in Environmental Policy and Ethics. I'm drawn to the places where molecules meet medicine — where a protein's shape might unlock a new therapy, or where data from a mass spectrometer tells a story about aging.

My research background spans proteomics and mass spectrometry, antibody engineering, immunohistochemistry, and cell culture. I've worked at the intersection of translational science and longevity, and I'm always looking for the next question worth asking.

Outside the lab, I think about design, sustainability, and the ethics of emerging technologies. I build things — software, ideas, arguments — and I try to make them beautiful as well as useful.
