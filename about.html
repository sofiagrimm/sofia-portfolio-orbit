About me

Hi, I'm Sofia.

I'm a student at Yale University pursuing a B.S. in Chemistry and Ethics, Politics & Economics (Class of 2029), with a GPA of 3.83. I grew up in Chicago and I'm drawn to the places where science meets systems — whether that's a protein's folding behavior, a disease mechanism, or a policy question about emerging technology.

My research spans neurodegenerative disease, top-down proteomics, and Drosophila genetics. Currently I'm at the Higley Lab at Yale School of Medicine, where I'm characterizing locus coeruleus axon degeneration in PPT1-deficient mice to investigate norepinephrine dysfunction in Parkinson's disease. I also work on the PCDD startup at Yale Engineering, developing business strategy for a patent-pending medication disposal device.

Outside the lab I consult, build, and write. I'm a Client Lead at Yale Healthcare Consulting Collective, an Associate Consultant at Global Strategy Consulting, and an Online Editor at the Yale Undergraduate Law Journal. I speak English, Chinese, Spanish, and Latin.
